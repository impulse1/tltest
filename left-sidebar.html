Influences

His mother exposed him to a wide variety of arts as a child and Tommy Love grew up inspired by the likes of Michael Jackson, Stevie Wonder, and Prince. He began dancing at an early age taking tap, jazz and gymnastics classes before even learning the power of his voice. Honing his skills as a stage performer in production plays and state choirs, his dreams as an entertainer began to take shape. During high school, Tommy Love began work on his singing and fell in love with the art. Music became his dream and passion..

About Tommy

Greatness is not this wonderful, esoteric, elusive, god like feature that only the special among us will ever taste. It’s something that truly exists in all of us. It’s very simple; this is what I believe, and I’m willing to die for it. This statement alone is enough to solidify Tommy as an artist. The first thought that might come to mind when observing him is his ridiculous, sickening work ethic. Or maybe it’s his ability to finesse any lyrics into a captivating melody. Whatever your thoughts may be, the simple fact remains; Tommy Love was born to sing and write records. On August 2, 1992, Thomas Cristobal Sanchez better know by his stage name Tommy Love was born in New Orleans, Louisiana. Tommy Love moved to Slovenia as a young boy. Tommy Love grew up loving music and throughout his childhood both in Slovenia and in The United States found time to appreciate it and learn from it. The summer following High School he attended classes at Berklee College of Music, sharpening his writing skills and learning the technical aspects of creating music.While there he connected with some musicians and joined a 4-part harmony group looking for a 5th member called Infinity , a modern day Boyz II Men. The group was short lived though and eventually disbanded, but Tommy Love's drive was not swayed. Even more motivated by the learning experience with Infinity and taste of recording and performing music, Tommy Love deferred his full scholarship to college for a year to focus solely on being an entertainer.

In 2007 Tommy Love was one of the finalists on the Hit MTV show "Making the Band." For its 4th season the show was looking to create an RnB group, thousands of people auditioned but in the end only 20 were selected to be on the show and have a chance to work with Diddy. Tommy Love was cut from the show but not without making some friends and connections that he keeps to this day. The resulting group was Day26 and Donnie Klang was offered a solo contract from the show. Tommy Love continued to do solo work though and has yet to do his best work. He is constantly recording and working on his craft in an attempt to continue to entertain and make music that he can be proud of.
